Cycle 1 contains our second project for Digital Fabrication. Once again, learning a new software in order to design and 3-D print a project with specific criteria. We were required to create something that is structured and operates like a container. The only rules pertain to the holding of the object. The object must fit snuggly and have no jostle when the container moves around.

For my project, because I had previous knowledge of Fusion 360 I wanted to design something different that served the purpose of the project, but had some creativeness as well. I had started to think about a lightbulb, and what you could do with a lightbulb. So I began designing a lightbulb that the lower half would unscrew and hold the object within the lightbulb case.

After designing the lightbulb container on Fusion 360 I downloaded the files as .STL files and uploaded them to Prusaslicer. Once positioning the bodies of the object correctly and adding supports where needed I downloaded the Gcode. I then uploaded the Gcode to an SD card to share with the 3-D printer.

I designed a lightbulb container that holds a flashdrive sucurely within. I made three iterations of this design to come to the final product holden the flashdrive without any jostling when the container is thrown or shook.
