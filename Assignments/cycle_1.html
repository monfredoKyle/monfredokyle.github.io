Cycle 1 contains our first project for Digital Fabrication. Starting out with no knowledge of the software, nor experience with the machines we were tasked with creating a lazer cut puzzle.

For my project, I had to learn how to use Adobe Illistrator. Having a quick tutorial on the software I created a artboard, or layout with the dimension's specific to my design and picture.

Secondly, I found what picture I wnted to use. Making the picture fit into the artboard was easy using control shift to keep the picture symetrical. Next I rasterized the picture using the objects tab in Adobe Illistrator. The rasterize function converts vectors into bitmap images. Lastly, using the windows tab in Adobe Illistrator. The image trace function clusters the newly formed pixels making any cut by the lazer cutter more precise.

In this step I found a puzzle generator on google. Once I found the puzzle pattern pleasing to my design I copied the code and pasted it into Adobe Illistrator. Then I converted the puzzle pieces to vector cuts.

All three of these steps combines into this image.

The final step will be to lazer cut this puzzle design. Hopefully down the road I'll get some experience with the machinery and be able to document the final outcome.
